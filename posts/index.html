PowerShell and `r`n

In short: CR is the escape character \r (carriage return, 0x0D, 13 in decimal) LF is the escape character \n (line feed, 0x0A, 10 in decimal) First let’s make two files for testing. One file with CRLF and one with LF. PS> mkdir c:\temp\ -EA SilentlyContinue PS> cd c:\temp PS> ([byte[]][char[]]"some") + ([byte[]] (13,10)) + ([byte[]][char[]]"testing") | Set-Content fileCrLf.txt -asByte PS> ([byte[]][char[]]"some") + [byte]10 + ([byte[]][char[]]"testing") | Set-Content fileLf.
